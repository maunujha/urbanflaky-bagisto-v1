# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Young, value-conscious shoppers (men & women) in India buying polo t-shirts and slim-fit casuals (₹299-799), browsing predominantly on mobile, looking for trend-forward basics at affordable prices. Success = a smooth browse-to-checkout journey with a premium feel that justifies the brand over generic marketplaces.

## Product Purpose

Urbanflaky (by Gabha Enterprise, Dholpur, Rajasthan) is a fashion e-commerce storefront built on Bagisto. The site exists to make affordable casualwear feel premium — homepage, lookbook, PDP storytelling, and the blog carry the brand identity, while cart/checkout/account stay functional but visually consistent with the same dark+neon system. Success looks like shoppers perceiving Urbanflaky as a confident, design-led brand rather than a budget marketplace, translating into completed checkouts.

## Brand Personality

Bold, edgy, premium streetwear. The existing dark theme (near-black `uf-bg` #0a0a0a, surfaces #141414/#1c1c1c, neon yellow-green accent #c7eb31) plus Poppins (body/UI) and DM Serif Display (display headings) already encode this — high contrast, confident, a touch of attitude, without tipping into cluttered or juvenile.

## Anti-references

- Generic warm-cream/sand "AI default" SaaS aesthetics — Urbanflaky is dark and high-contrast, not soft and neutral.
- Cluttered marketplace UI (Myntra/Ajio-style density, banner overload, gradient-text sales badges).
- Corporate/minimal-to-the-point-of-flat — the brand needs edge and energy, not sterile restraint.
- Side-stripe accent borders, gradient text, generic numbered eyebrows/section markers — standard impeccable bans, doubly so here since the neon accent already does the "pop" work.

## Design Principles

- **Dark is the canvas, neon is the punctuation** — `uf-accent` (#c7eb31) is used deliberately for CTAs and key moments, not smeared across the UI.
- **Storytelling sells the basics** — lookbook, blog, and PDP imagery/copy carry the "premium streetwear" feel that the ₹299-799 price point alone wouldn't convey.
- **Mobile-first, thumb-first** — most traffic is mobile; layouts, tap targets, and motion must hold up on small screens before desktop.
- **Consistency across registers** — transactional flows (cart/checkout/account) inherit the same tokens (`uf-*` classes, Poppins/DM Serif) so the brand never feels like it's handed off to a different system mid-journey.
- **Earn the edge, don't force it** — bold doesn't mean noisy; contrast and confidence come from typography scale, spacing, and the accent color, not from piling on effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard best practice: WCAG AA contrast for body text against the dark backgrounds (`uf-bg`/`uf-surface`/`uf-surface2`), `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animations, no color-only signals (the neon accent must be paired with text/icons/labels, not relied on alone — relevant given color-blind users may not distinguish it from the dark palette).
